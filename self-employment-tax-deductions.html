📝 Top 15 Self-Employment Tax Deductions (2026)

Every dollar you deduct = less tax. Here's what freelancers miss most.

1. Home Office Deduction

Deduct $5 per square foot (simplified method) up to 300 sq ft, OR actual expenses (mortgage interest, utilities, insurance). Must be used regularly and exclusively for work.

Average savings: $1,500/year

2. Health Insurance Premiums

Self-employed? Deduct 100% of medical, dental, and long-term care insurance premiums for you and your family.

Average savings: $4,000–8,000/year

3. SEP IRA or Solo 401(k) Contributions

Contribute up to 25% of your net self-employment income (max $69,000 for 2026). Reduces both income tax and AGI.

Retirement + tax savings combo

4. Business Meals (50%)

Meals with clients, prospects, or business meetings. Keep receipts with notes on business purpose.

Up to $2,000+ deduction

5. Internet & Phone Bills

Deduct percentage used for business. Track usage for 2 weeks to calculate ratio.

6. Software & Subscriptions

Adobe Creative Cloud, QuickBooks, Canva Pro, Zoom, Calendly — fully deductible.

7. Continuing Education

Courses, conferences, certifications, eBooks related to your freelance skill.

8. Vehicle Mileage (Standard 67¢/mile for 2026)

Drive to client meetings, picking up supplies, bank runs for business. Log every trip.

9. Bank & Transaction Fees

PayPal, Stripe, Square fees + business bank account monthly fees.

10. Legal & Professional Services

CPA fees, attorney fees, contract review services.

11. Office Supplies & Equipment

Laptop, monitor, printer, paper, pens — Section 179 allows full deduction up to $1.16M.

12. Marketing & Advertising

Google Ads, social media ads, business cards, website hosting, SEO tools.

13. Shipping & Postage

If you ship physical products to clients, track every label.

14. Business Insurance

Liability insurance, E&O insurance, equipment insurance.

15. Qualified Business Income Deduction (Section 199A)

Deduct up to 20% of your qualified business income if your taxable income is under $191,950 (single) or $383,900 (married).

MASSIVE deduction — up to $10k+

Quick Tip

Use separate bank account and credit card for business expenses. Saves hours at tax time.
